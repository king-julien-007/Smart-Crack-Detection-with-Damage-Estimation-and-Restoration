Structural cracks may occur due to aging, environmental stress, or excessive load. Manual
inspection is time-consuming and subjective. To address this, the project uses VGG16 with transfer learning to automatically detect cracks from images. XAI is integrated to improve transparency
by highlighting the regions influencing predictions. The framework further includes restoration of
detected crack areas and estimation of crack dimensions, providing a complete automated solution
for structural health monitoring.[1] To further refine the structural health monitoring framework,
crack restoration is achieved through depth-aware image inpainting, where the binary mask generated by the VGG16 model identifies the target area for removal. By utilizing a depth AI model,
such as a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) or Deep Image Prior informed by spatial contours, the system fills the fissured regions with synthesized textures that match the surrounding
healthy material. This restoration maintains geometric consistency by using depth data to ensure
that lighting and perspective align with the 3D orientation of the structure, effectively creating a
"digital twin" of the building in its undamaged state. Simultaneously, depth estimation enables the
transition from qualitative detection to quantitative dimensional analysis. By mapping the Z-axis,
the framework calculates the real-world width and length of cracks using the relationship between
the pixel count, the focal length of the camera, and the estimated distance from the sensor.This
allows for precise measurement of crack severity and the calculation of volumetric data, providing
engineers with exact metrics for the volume of repair materials required and the overall structural
risk.
